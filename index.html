Jack Yi Yang

I am a SLAM engineer at PHIAR, a startup focusing on combining augmented reality, road understanding, and in-car navigation. Check out our latest collaboration here.

I obtained my Master degree in the Robotics Institute, Carnegie Mellon University in 2019. I worked with Prof. Michael Kaess in the Robot Perception Lab. My research interest lies in the field of robotics, and I am particularly in 3D reconstruction, SLAM, and state estimation.

I graduated from Harvey Mudd College with a Bachelor degree in engineering in 2017. I worked at the Lab for Autonomous and Intelligent Robotics with Prof. Christopher Clark. I had a great time working on algorithms in multi-robot system and path planning.

I worked at Apple as a summer intern in 2016, where I worked on pose estimation using portable inertial sensors in the CoreMotion team.
